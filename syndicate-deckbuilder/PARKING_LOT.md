# Syndicate Deckbuilder System: Parking Lot

This is a space for brainstorming, ideas, and notes for the **Syndicate Deckbuilder** system. Items here are either not yet formalized in the [CoreRules.md](../rules/CoreRules.md) or represent open design gaps.

## 🧪 Open Design Gaps

### Core Systems

- **Semi-Cooperative Elements:** How do players interact with shared threats or tension? Should there be a "Global Tension" pool in addition to individual tension?
- **Market Churn:** How do we prevent the planning phase market from stagnating if no one buys the visible cards? (e.g., sweeping the whole row paying a resource, or the oldest card drops off each round).
- **Escalation:** Does the central encounter or environment escalate as time passes? If so, what is the trigger (round count, tension threshold, etc.)?

### Extraction Phase

- **Extraction Choices:** Choices around *how* players extract (e.g., A fast route guarantees high Tension or loss of some rewards; A slow route requires specific skill checks).
- **Extraction Challenge:** Number and types of extraction challenges. How does the "degree of success" mathematically affect reward loss?

## 💡 Brainstorming: Specialist Skills

Specialists define the asymmetrical starting positions. Potential skill focuses include:

- **Improved Currency:** Increased value for acquiring market cards with a certain currency type.
- **Alternate Market Acquisition:** Methods for acquiring cards without straightforward currency.
- **Challenge Type Specialization:** Bonuses when matching specific challenge requirements.
- **Challenge Circumvention:** Alternate methods for resolving challenges.
- **Deck Manipulation:** Thinning, searching, or cycling the player's deck.
- **Market Manipulation:** Searching or cycling the market.
- **Zone Manipulation:** Modifying the mission or reward decks.
- **Extraction Specialization:** Bonuses specifically for the Extraction phase.
- **Team Synergy:** Granting bonuses or currencies to other players.
- **Layaway / Scheming:** Reserving market cards for later acquisition.

## 🛠️ Card Mechanics Ideas

Items below may be refined or added to the formal mechanics list.

- **Intel:** Cards that reveal upcoming challenges or modify the "Mission Deck."
- **Synchronicity:** Bonus effects when played in tandem with other archetypes.
- **Burn/Exhaust:** Powerful one-time use cards permanently removed after play.
- **Tableau Builders:** Permanent assets providing passive bonuses.
- **Deck Pollution (Wounds/Stress):** Negative cards added to the deck from failed challenges or risky actions.
- **Flashbacks:** Cards that explain how characters "prepared" for a specific obstacle, perhaps allowing for retrospective resource spending.

## ⚖️ Balance & Meta-Design

- **Asymmetry vs. Fairness:** Experiment with specialists having unique vs. common starting cards.
- **RNG Threshold:** Defining the "Luck vs. Skill" balance in challenge resolution.
- **VP Weighting:** How much should specific rewards be worth relative to the difficulty of extracting them?

## 🎨 Thematic Mappings

The core structure is theme-agnostic, but these mappings can help ground the mechanics:

- **Heist (The Original):** Recruiting (Crew Selection) -> Planning (Gathering Intel/Gear) -> Execution (The Job) -> Extraction (The Getaway).
- **Cyberpunk:** Recruiting (Hiring Operatives) -> Planning (Buying Software/ICE-Breakers) -> Execution (The Run) -> Extraction (Jack Out/Exfiltration).
- **Fantasy Exploration:** Recruiting (Adventurer Guild) -> Planning (Buying Potions/Maps) -> Execution (Dungeon Dive) -> Extraction (Escaping the Trap/Encounter).
- **Political Intrigue:** Recruiting (Building a Cabinet) -> Planning (Bribes/Blackmail) -> Execution (The Coup/Election) -> Extraction (Managing the Fallout/Consolidating Power).

## ⚡ Advanced Mechanics Considerations

### Specialized Tension

- **Tension Caps:** Should there be a "safe" amount of Tension? I think this can be handled by having a higher tension floor for penalties.
- **Public vs. Private Tension:** Does your Tension affect everyone (Global) or just your own Extraction difficulty? Tension should be private for each player to manage. Players decide to take specialist packs or market cards that generate more heat than others. They should be the ones to take any penalty for it.
- **Tension Burn:** Ways to reduce Tension during Execution (e.g., "Covering your tracks"). I think this could be tied to specialist ability.

### Extraction Depth

- **Forced Abandonment:** Instead of just VP loss, do you lose cards? Extraction is the end of the game. The only card loss that would matter is ones with VP values.
- **Last Stand:** A mechanic where a player can risk everything to keep a reward, potentially resulting in high VP or total failure.

### Dual Currency Flavor

- **Material vs. Social:** Gold vs. Reputation.
- **Speed vs. Silence:** Action points vs. Steatlh points.
- **Asset vs. Access:** Physical equipment vs. Security clearance.
