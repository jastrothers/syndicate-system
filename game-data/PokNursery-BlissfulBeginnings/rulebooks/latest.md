## Overview

**Players:** 2-4 | **Ages:** 8+ | **Duration:** 45-60 minutes | **Weight:** Light-Medium (Family)

In the pastoral highlands east of Goldenrod City, the Blissful Downs is where licensed nursery keepers raise baby Pokemon until they evolve. Each spring, rangers bring newly hatched babies to the Welcoming Meadow. You are one of these nursery keepers. Under the guidance of a seasoned Nursery Manager, you will welcome baby Pokemon into your cradles, tend to their unique needs, and experience the joy of watching them undergo their Evolutionary Moment.

The keeper who demonstrates the greatest excellence in care across the season earns the Golden Egg Trophy. Victory is measured in Gratitude.

**How You Win:** Over 9 rounds (divided into 3 Season Arcs), adopt baby Pokemon, place Care Tokens on them to fulfill their Growth Recipes, evolve them into their next forms, and score Gratitude. The player with the most Gratitude at the end of Round 9 (or when a player triggers the early end condition) wins.

## Thematic Brief

# PokéNursery: Blissful Beginnings — Thematic Specification

## I. NARRATIVE FOUNDATION

### The Story

In the pastoral highlands east of Goldenrod City, there exists a chain of rolling meadows known as the **Blissful Downs** — a place where wild Chansey have gathered since ancient times to tend to abandoned and orphaned eggs. Over generations, the Pokémon League formalized this tradition into the **Blissful Beginnings Program**, a network of licensed nurseries where trained caregivers raise baby Pokémon until they are strong, happy, and loved enough to achieve their first evolution.

Each spring, rangers from across Johto bring newly hatched baby Pokémon to the Downs. They arrive in woven baskets carried on the backs of Stantler, delivered to the **Welcoming Meadow** — the central clearing where nursery keepers choose which little ones to take under their wing. The babies are fragile, curious, and full of potential, but they cannot evolve on their own. They need someone to feed them, play with them, and make sure they get enough rest. They need a nursery.

**You are one of these nursery keepers.** Under the guidance of a seasoned Nursery Manager — a Chansey, Blissey, Wigglytuff, or Audino who has devoted their life to this work — you will build your nursery from a humble clearing into a thriving sanctuary. You will welcome baby Pokémon into your cradles, tend to their unique needs, and experience the joy of watching them glow with evolutionary light when they are finally ready.

### The Stakes

The Blissful Beginnings Program awards its highest honor — the **Golden Egg Trophy** — to the nursery keeper who best demonstrates excellence in care across the season. This is not a competition of speed or aggression; it is a recognition of attentiveness, harmony, and devotion. The keeper who evolves the most Pokémon, raises them in balanced harmony, and builds the most nurturing environment will earn the right to open a permanent nursery on the Blissful Downs.

### What Happens When They Evolve?

When a baby Pokémon's needs are fully met, it undergoes its **Evolutionary Moment** — a radiant burst of light as it transforms into its next form. The evolved Pokémon is moved to the nursery's **Sunshine Garden**, a sheltered grove where graduates play together while awaiting their new trainers. Every evolved Pokémon in the Sunshine Garden is a testament to the keeper's skill and a source of pride.

### Tone

Warm. Gentle. Hopeful. This is a game about care, not combat. The tension comes from managing limited time and attention across multiple needy babies, not from attacking other players. Think of the feeling of a sunlit morning in a garden, the sound of small creatures chirping contentedly, the satisfaction of watching something you nurtured grow into something wonderful.

## II. PLAYER IDENTITY

Each player is a **Nursery Keeper** — a human caregiver who has apprenticed under one of four legendary Pokémon Nursery Managers. At the start of the game, players draft their Nursery Manager in reverse player order.

### The Four Nursery Managers

**Chansey — "The Egg Whisperer"** — Free Nourish each turn; draw 1 Provisions card on evolution.
**Blissey — "The Joykeeper"** — Free Lullaby each turn; all Delight requirements reduced by 1.
**Wigglytuff — "The Dreamweaver"** — Free Lullaby each turn; swap 2 Care Tokens between babies once per round.
**Audino — "The Keen Listener"** — Free Frolic each turn; Forage draws 3 instead of 2.

## III. MECHANISM-TO-THEME TRANSLITERATION

### Care Points (Action Points): 3 CP/turn
- **Nourish** (1 CP): Place 1 Berry token on a baby.
- **Frolic** (1 CP): Place 1 Delight token on a baby.
- **Lullaby** (1 CP): Place 1 Slumber token on a baby.
- **Forage for Provisions** (1 CP): Draw 2 Provisions cards.
- **Welcome to the Nursery** (1 CP): Take 1 baby from the Welcoming Meadow.
- **Open the Nursery Door** (0 CP, 1x/turn): Activate a Nursery Room ability.

### Growth Recipes (Set Collection)
Each baby has a Growth Recipe of 5-7 Care Tokens (Berry + Delight + Slumber). Provisions cards substitute at 2:1. Kinship Bond: +2 Gratitude for same-type evolved pairs.

### The Three Gatherings (Drafting)
- **The Welcoming Meadow**: 4 face-up baby Pokémon cards.
- **The Morning Caravan**: 4 Provisions cards at round start, 1 free each.
- **The Builder's Fair**: Nursery Room tiles at rounds 3, 6, 9 in reverse score order.

### Your Nursery (Tableau Building)
- **The Cradle Row**: 4 slots for baby Pokémon.
- **Nursery Rooms**: 3 upgrade slots.
- **The Manager's Den**: 1 manager slot.
- **The Sunshine Garden**: Scoring zone for evolved Pokémon.

### The Rhythm of the Nursery (Income)
- **Morning Routine**: 1 Berry + 1 Delight + 1 Slumber each round.
- **Manager's Touch**: +1 specialty token per round.
- **Nursery Comforts**: Token-generating room bonuses.
- **Reputation of Care**: +1 Provisions card per evolved Pokémon per round.

Three Season Arcs: First Light (1-3), Midday Sun (4-6), Golden Hour (7-9).

1. **Dawn Phase** — Morning Routine income.

## Setup

Perform these steps in order:

**1. Assemble Central Area:** Place the Round Tracker, Score Track tiles, Welcoming Meadow tile, Morning Caravan tile, Builder's Fair tile, and 3 Season Arc Cards. Place the Round Marker on space 1.

**2. Baby Pokemon Deck:** Shuffle all 34 Baby cards face-down. Place beside the Meadow. Draw 4 face-up into the Meadow display.

**3. Evolved Pokemon Deck:** Stack all 34 Evolved cards face-down nearby (sort alphabetically for easy lookup).

**4. Provisions Deck:** Shuffle all 48 Provisions cards face-down. Place beside the Morning Caravan.

**5. Nursery Room Tiles:** Shuffle all 8 tiles face-down in a stack near the Builder's Fair tile.

**6. Token Supply:** Place all 120 Care Tokens (40 Berry, 40 Delight, 40 Slumber) in separate piles. Place all 28 Gratitude Tokens near the Score Track.

**7. Nursery Star Cards:** Place the 3 Star cards face-up beside the Score Track.

**8. First Player:** The player who most recently cared for a real animal takes the First Keeper Marker. Otherwise, youngest player.

**9. Manager Draft:** Lay out all 4 Managers face-up. Starting with the player to the RIGHT of the First Keeper (counterclockwise), each player selects one. Return unchosen Managers to the box.

**10. Player Boards:** Each player takes a Nursery Board, places the Lock Overlay on the 5th Cradle slot, places their Score Marker on 0, and takes their Reference Card and trackers.

**11. Starting Resources:** Each player receives 2 Berry, 2 Delight, 2 Slumber tokens and draws 2 Provisions cards (kept secret).

**12. Initial Baby:** Starting with the First Keeper clockwise, each player selects 1 Baby from the Meadow and places it in a Cradle. Refill the Meadow to 4 after each selection. The game begins with Round 1.

## Game Structure

The game is played over 9 rounds in 3 Season Arcs:

| Season Arc | Rounds | Builder's Fair |
|------------|--------|----------------|
| Arc I: First Light | 1-3 | End of Round 3 |
| Arc II: Midday Sun | 4-6 | End of Round 6 |
| Arc III: Golden Hour | 7-9 | End of Round 9 |

Each round has 5 phases in order:
1. Dawn Phase (Morning Routine) -- Simultaneous
2. Caravan Phase (Morning Caravan) -- Turn Order
3. Care Phase (The Heart of the Day) -- Turn Order
4. Twilight Phase (Evolutionary Moments) -- Simultaneous
5. Nightfall Phase (Cleanup)

## Round Structure (Detailed)

### Phase 1: Dawn Phase -- Morning Routine (Simultaneous)
Each player collects:
- **Base Income:** 1 Berry + 1 Delight + 1 Slumber
- **Manager's Morning Bonus:** +1 token matching Manager's free action type (Chansey: Berry, Blissey/Wigglytuff: Slumber, Audino: Delight)
- **Nursery Room Income:** +1 per token-generating room (Oran Orchard: Berry, Giggle Grounds: Delight, Moonlit Hollow: Slumber)
- **Evolution Momentum:** Draw 1 Provisions card per evolved Pokemon in your Sunshine Garden
- Players may also play Provisions cards during Dawn for additional tokens.

### Phase 2: Caravan Phase -- Morning Caravan (Turn Order)
Reveal 4 Provisions cards face-up (3 in 2p). Starting with the First Keeper clockwise, each player takes 1 card free. Remaining cards stay until Nightfall discard. If the deck is empty, reshuffle the discard pile.

### Phase 3: Care Phase -- The Heart of the Day (Turn Order)
Starting with the First Keeper clockwise, each player takes a full turn: spend 3 Care Points on actions (any order, repeats allowed except Nursery Door), plus 1 free Manager action and 1 free Nursery Door activation. Unspent CP are lost. Provisions cards may be played freely throughout the turn at no CP cost.

### Phase 4: Twilight Phase -- Evolutionary Moments (Simultaneous)
All players check cradles for completed Growth Recipes. For each completed baby: (1) return all tokens to supply, (2) remove Baby card to box, (3) place matching Evolved card in Sunshine Garden, (4) apply evolution bonuses (Chansey draw, Chime Tower token). Check Star of Swiftness. Refill Meadow to 4 (3 in 2p).

### Phase 5: Nightfall Phase -- Cleanup
1. Discard remaining Caravan cards. 2. Builder's Fair on rounds 3, 6, 9. 3. Pass First Keeper Marker clockwise. 4. Advance Round Marker.

## Actions

During the Care Phase, spend 3 Care Points on these actions:

| Action | Cost | Effect |
|--------|------|--------|
| Nourish | 1 CP | Place 1 Berry token from your supply onto any baby in your Cradle Row |
| Frolic | 1 CP | Place 1 Delight token from your supply onto any baby in your Cradle Row |
| Lullaby | 1 CP | Place 1 Slumber token from your supply onto any baby in your Cradle Row |
| Forage for Provisions | 1 CP | Draw 2 Provisions cards from the deck (Audino draws 3) |
| Welcome to the Nursery | 1 CP | Take 1 baby from the Welcoming Meadow to an empty Cradle slot; refill Meadow |
| Open the Nursery Door | 0 CP, 1x/turn | Activate 1 Nursery Room tile you own (only The Tender Bridge requires activation) |

**Token Placement:** You must have the token in your personal supply. You may place on any baby, including one adopted this turn.

**Capacity:** Cradle Row holds 4 babies (5 with The Fifth Cradle). If full, you cannot Welcome. You may never voluntarily remove a baby.

**Provisions Cards:** Typed Provisions (Berry/Delight/Slumber) may be played from hand at any time during your turn (or Dawn Phase) to gain 1 matching token -- free, no CP cost. Any 2 Provisions cards may be discarded for 1 token of any type (2:1 wildcard) -- also free. No hand limit.

**Deck Exhaustion:** If the Provisions Deck is empty when you Forage, shuffle the discard pile to form a new deck. If both are empty, Forage is unavailable. If the Baby Deck and Meadow are both empty, Welcome is unavailable.

### The Wishing Egg (Revised)

At game end, score +1 Gratitude for each evolved Pokémon in your Sunshine Garden beyond your first two. (Example: 4 evolutions = +2 Gratitude; 5 evolutions = +3 Gratitude; 2 or fewer = +0.)

## Builder's Fair

Occurs at end of Rounds 3, 6, and 9 during Nightfall Phase.

**Procedure:**
1. Reveal (player count + 1) tiles face-up: 3 tiles (2p), 4 tiles (3p), 5 tiles (4p).
2. Draft in REVERSE score order (lowest Gratitude picks first -- catch-up mechanism). Tie-breaking: tied player furthest clockwise from First Keeper drafts first.
3. Each player takes 1 tile and places it on an empty Nursery Room slot. If all 3 slots are full, a player may replace an existing tile (returned to box). The Fifth Cradle's unlock is permanent even if replaced.
4. Remaining tiles return to the bottom of the tile stack (not the box).

**Tile Availability:** Round 3 Fair draws from all 8 tiles. Round 6 and 9 Fairs draw from remaining tiles. If fewer tiles remain than needed, reveal all that remain.

## Scoring -- The Golden Egg Ceremony

| Source | Gratitude |
|--------|-----------|
| Evolved Pokemon | 3, 4, or 5 each (printed value) |
| Kinship Bond (pair sharing a type) | +2 each (+3 with Bonded Ribbon) |
| The Wishing Egg (if owned) | +1 per evolved Pokemon |
| Leftover Care Tokens (personal supply only) | +1 per 3 tokens (any mix) |
| Star of Devotion (most evolutions) | +2 (shared if tied) |
| Star of Harmony (most different types) | +2 (shared if tied) |
| Star of Swiftness (first to 3 evolutions) | +2 |

**Kinship Bonds:** Each pair of evolved Pokemon sharing at least one type scores +2 (+3 with Bonded Ribbon). Dual-type Pokemon can bond via either type. Each Pokemon can be in multiple pairs. Count each unique pair once.

**Example:** Clefairy (Fairy) + Togetic (Fairy) + Jigglypuff (Normal/Fairy) with Bonded Ribbon = 3 pairs x 3 = 9 Gratitude.

**Leftover Tokens:** Only unplaced tokens in personal supply count. Tokens on unevolved babies are lost.

**Star of Harmony Type Counting:** Each unique type counts once. Dual-type Pokemon contribute both types.

**Examples:**
- Player has Pikachu (Elec), Electabuzz (Elec), Jynx (Ice/Psychic). Kinship Bonds: Pikachu+Electabuzz = +2. Total bond Gratitude: 2.
- Player with Bonded Ribbon has Clefairy (Fairy), Togetic (Fairy), Jigglypuff (Normal/Fairy), Marill (Water). Bonds: Clefairy+Togetic (+3), Clefairy+Jigglypuff (+3), Togetic+Jigglypuff (+3). Total: 9.

## Game End

**Standard End:** After Round 9's Nightfall Phase (including the final Builder's Fair), proceed to the Golden Egg Ceremony.

**Early End Trigger:** If any player completes their 6th evolution (5th in 2-player), finish the current round completely (including Nightfall and any Builder's Fair), then proceed to scoring. No additional rounds are played.

**Winner:** Highest Gratitude total wins. Tiebreakers in order: (1) most evolved Pokemon, (2) most Nursery Star cards, (3) shared victory.

## Quick Reference -- Turn Sequence

**Dawn (Simultaneous):** Collect base income (1B+1D+1S) + Manager bonus + Room income + Evolution Momentum draws. Play Provisions optionally.

**Caravan (Turn Order):** Reveal 4 cards (3 in 2p). Each player drafts 1 free.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I place tokens on a baby I just adopted this turn?** A: Yes.

**Q: What happens to tokens on unevolved babies at game end?** A: They are lost. Only unplaced tokens in personal supply count for scoring.

**Q: Can I over-feed a baby?** A: Yes. Excess tokens are legal but returned to supply on evolution.

**Q: What if the General Supply runs out of a token type?** A: You cannot place that type. Wait for evolutions to return tokens.

**Q: Can Wigglytuff swap tokens to/from the supply?** A: No, only between two babies in your Cradle Row.

**Q: Does Blissey's ability affect newly adopted babies?** A: Yes, it applies to all babies for the entire game.

**Q: Can I abandon a baby to make room?** A: No. Wait for an evolution or acquire The Fifth Cradle.

**Q: When is the Provisions discard reshuffled?** A: Whenever you need to draw and the deck is empty.

**Q: How does Tyrogue's evolution work?** A: Player chooses Hitmonlee, Hitmonchan, or Hitmontop. All are Fighting type.

**Q: Can I have two copies of the same evolved Pokemon?** A: Yes. They form a Kinship Bond with each other.

**Q: What if fewer tiles remain for a Builder's Fair?** A: Reveal all remaining and draft as normal. If none remain, skip the Fair.

## Balance Errata (v1.1)

The following balance adjustments were applied based on adversarial critique analysis:

**F1 — Wishing Egg Rebalance:** The Wishing Egg now reads: '+1 Gratitude for each evolved Pokémon beyond your first two in the Sunshine Garden.' A player with 4 evolutions gains +2 (not +4). This brings it in line with income rooms.

**F2 — Evolution Momentum Cap:** Evolution Momentum is capped at +2 Provisions cards per round, regardless of how many evolved Pokémon are in the Sunshine Garden. This prevents snowballing.

**F3 — Provisions Hand Limit:** Maximum hand size is 5 Provisions cards. Discard down to 5 at the end of your Care Phase. This applies to all players.

**F4 — Kinship Bond Duplicate Ruling:** Kinship Bonds only apply between two DIFFERENT species that share a type. Two copies of the same evolved species (e.g., two Pikachu) do NOT form a Kinship Bond with each other. They count as one unique species for Bond purposes.

**F5 — Chansey Buff:** Once per round, when Chansey's keeper places a Berry token via Nourish (paid or free), they may place 1 additional Berry token on the same baby at no CP cost.

**F6 — Wigglytuff Rework:** Wigglytuff's special ability is replaced: 'Once per round, after another player adopts a baby from the Welcoming Meadow, you may immediately adopt 1 baby from the Meadow for free (0 CP). This ignores the normal 1-adoption-per-action limit but still requires an empty Cradle slot.'

**F7 — Blissey Adjustment:** Blissey's Delight reduction (-1, min 1) now applies only to the first baby you evolve each round. Subsequent evolutions in the same Twilight Phase use the printed Delight requirement.

## Economy Rules

**Provisions Hand Limit:** Your hand of Provisions cards may never exceed 5. At the end of your Care Phase, if you hold more than 5 Provisions cards, discard down to 5 (your choice which to discard). This limit does not apply during the Dawn Phase or Caravan Phase — only checked at Care Phase end.

**Evolution Momentum Cap:** During the Dawn Phase, draw Provisions cards for Evolution Momentum up to a maximum of 2 cards, regardless of how many evolved Pokémon are in your Sunshine Garden.

**Kinship Bond Clarification:** Kinship Bonds only form between two DIFFERENT species that share at least one type. Two copies of the same evolved Pokémon (e.g., two Pikachu) do not form a Kinship Bond with each other.
